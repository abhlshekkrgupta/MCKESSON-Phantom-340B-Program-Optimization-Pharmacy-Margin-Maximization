## Key Compliance Considerations

### Patient Qualification

The six-part patient definition test is established in HRSA guidance and has been refined through audit findings and advisory opinions. Each covered entity must maintain auditable documentation demonstrating that every 340B-eligible prescription meets all six criteria. Common audit deficiencies include failure to document the patient's relationship with the covered entity, prescriptions from providers not employed by or under contract with the covered entity, and services provided outside the covered entity's scope of grant.

### Medicaid Duplicate Discount Prevention

Covered entities must have a system to prevent Medicaid duplicate discounts. This is consistently the most common HRSA audit finding. Entities must either carve out Medicaid FFS claims from 340B eligibility or ensure that Medicaid rebates are not claimed on 340B drugs. For Medicaid MCO claims, the policy varies by state. Entities must verify each state's Medicaid MCO 340B policy before including MCO claims in their 340B program.

### GPO Prohibition

DSH hospitals, children's hospitals, and free-standing cancer hospitals that participate in 340B are prohibited from obtaining covered outpatient drugs through a Group Purchasing Organization. This prohibition applies at the drug level. An entity may use a GPO for non-covered drugs and for drugs administered to inpatients. The segregation must be demonstrable.

### Diversion Prevention

340B drugs may only be dispensed to eligible patients of the covered entity. Dispensing to ineligible patients constitutes diversion and can result in program termination. Common diversion scenarios include dispensing to patients whose prescriptions originate from non-covered entity providers, dispensing at non-registered sites, and failing to maintain auditable inventory records.

### Record Keeping

Covered entities must maintain auditable records for all 340B transactions for a period of not less than five years. Records must demonstrate compliance with patient qualification, duplicate discount prevention, GPO prohibition, and diversion prevention requirements. Inadequate record-keeping is itself an audit finding.

---

## HRSA Audit Process

HRSA conducts audits of covered entities on a rotating basis. Audits typically focus on patient qualification documentation, Medicaid duplicate discount prevention, GPO compliance, and diversion prevention. Findings are classified by severity. Critical findings may result in repayment obligations or program termination. Entities should conduct internal mock audits annually and maintain a remediation plan for any identified gaps.

---

## Regulatory Updates

The 340B program is subject to ongoing regulatory and legislative activity. Recent areas of focus include contract pharmacy restrictions, hospital eligibility criteria, and transparency requirements. Covered entities should monitor HRSA guidance and federal register notices for changes affecting their program operations.
